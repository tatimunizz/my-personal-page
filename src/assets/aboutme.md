# Who am I

I am a Computer Science student in my final year of college, following the Multimedia Computing specialization. My professional experience is in backend development: I built Restful APIs for chatbots that facilitated communication between companies and their customers, reaching over one million accesses and turning conversations into real sales.

Behind the endpoints and queries, there has always been a genuine passion for frontend and for the visible layer of things. I believe my technical background, combined with my creative eye, allows me to move between what works and what enchants. At the end of the day, programming is also creating.

Away from screens, you'll find me photographing the city with an analog camera, rearranging furniture at home, crocheting, or visiting cafés and bookstores with my husband. I am driven by small everyday inventions and a constant desire to experiment with new forms of expression.

# My professional learning path

My journey began in 2016 when I decided to start a technical course in game programming. I have always considered myself someone inclined toward the arts and humanities. My first choice for a degree was Portuguese Language, and I believed that I did not understand the world of numbers. But the game programming course showed me the opposite. In it, I learned to think critically about solutions to problems I didn't even know existed. I conditioned my mind to think in algorithms and to see the world through the filter of code. I understood that everything around me is mathematics, and that knowing how to program gave me the possibility to create whatever I could imagine. I really enjoyed making games, especially logic-based ones. Together with my classmates, I started a startup called Goa Studios, and we produced four games during the course and won a game jam.

During this experience I learned Java and, in turn, object-oriented programming. My opinion is that every programmer should start their learning with Java. It is a great language that very clearly applies the concepts needed to truly understand what is happening. After learning Java, I am sure that one can learn any other language. Today I have already programmed in C, C++, Python, JavaScript, and what changes is the syntax, but my way of seeing code was very well grounded in Java.

I also learned project management, the importance of having documentation and writing it clearly. I took design classes focused on games and digital interfaces, and that is where I get my courage to try my hand at web design. It was only 12 months of study, four games produced, but it shaped me as a programmer and gave me a solid foundation to deepen these studies in my Computer Science degree with determination and confidence.

In 2019 I discovered art in code and once again found myself enchanted by the possibilities of programming. I took online courses such as "Creative Coding in JS" by Bruno Imbrizi, learned a lot from the YouTube channel "The Coding Train" by Daniel Shiffman, and many others that gave me extensive knowledge of platforms like Processing, p5.js, canvas-sketch.js, and taught me to see numbers in many creative forms. At university, I had the pleasure of taking courses such as "Computer Vision" and "Multimedia Computing", where I had fun combining my prior knowledge of data visualization with the new knowledge acquired in these disciplines.

And speaking of university, 2020 was when I started my undergraduate degree at the University of Macau (UM), where I challenged myself a lot in math classes, and with each passing semester I became even more enchanted with this world of numbers. I dedicated myself to the disciplines of multimedia and "Advanced Database Systems". I enjoy learning different algorithms to solve different problems, and I also learned to condition my brain to think of algorithms to solve the problems I encounter along this learning journey. Surprisingly for me, among the mandatory subjects of the course, my favorites so far are "Linear Algebra", I believe I have no explanation for that, I just liked the subject and apply a lot of it to digital media, and "Principles of Operating Systems", which not only made me fascinated with OS creation, but the classes where we learned to manipulate hardware components in code excited me and made me develop a taste for electronics.

For a period, I helped with research at the Natural Language Processing & Portuguese-Chinese Machine Translatiuon Laboratory (NLP2CT), in a study on evaluation of cross-lingual LLM-generated text detection for Hindi, Telugu, and Tamil, where I assisted in data collection, learned about the research process, from the question to the experiment to writing the final paper.

I had to defer my studies for several years due to difficulties during the pandemic. I started a full‑time job as a back‑end developer at Zenvia, a digital solutions company from Brazil. I worked remotely for more than three years in the chatbot building area, where I developed my backend abilities and had a great time experiencing work life. That experience helped me become a better professional. Not only was I learning, but I was learning by doing, by talking to colleagues, and by observing their ways of working. On this journey, I got promoted to mid‑level programmer. I became an essential asset for my team's quality of delivery, and I got to join a few different teams that have clients in banking, retail sales, and insurance companies. Around the company, I was known as a person who solves problems, works efficiently, and knows how to communicate with client stakeholders.

As you can tell, I am a person who easily becomes enchanted by various topics, I am flexible and adapt to whatever is needed to dedicate myself willingly to what is necessary, but I do not set aside my tastes and hobbies, because I understand that being a person who creates in multiple areas makes me a more complete and versatile professional.

# My story

I was born in Rio de Janeiro. Since childhood, I carried an overflowing imagination. I created so many imaginary friends that my family became concerned. But I was lucky to grow up alongside an artist who never claimed to be one: my mother. She says she is just an art teacher, but I always saw her inventing murals for school parties, choreographing dances with students, and making jewelry with her best friend in her free time. A true multimedia artist, and my greatest inspiration.

Talking about my mother is talking about myself. I grew up dreaming of being an artist, but adult life first took me along other paths. At 16, I started working as a model, and after finishing high school, I dove into that career. This led me, at 18, to live and work in China, a leap that taught me to survive, communicate, and reinvent myself in a country where I spoke neither English nor Mandarin.

It was almost ten years of living off my image, until at 25 I felt I needed something different. That was when I rediscovered programming, which had already fascinated me for its creative possibilities. I enrolled in Computer Science, a course not always recognized for creativity, but one that revealed a path aligned with my tastes if I knew how to dig. And I dug.

Beyond following the obvious path nowadays, artificial intelligence, I dove into multimedia computing and the infinite ways of uniting code, visuals, and meaning. In Thailand, where I lived during the pandemic, I rediscovered drawing and had my first contact with generative art through Processing. It was a period of reconnecting with the part of myself that adult life had put on hold. I understood that creating, for me, is as vital as breathing.

Amid this journey, I met the love of my life at 19. My husband is a musician, writer, teacher, and a dreamer as restless as I am. Our worlds collided, and since then, our main goal has been simple and ambitious: to keep creating, together, as much as we can.

Maybe you don't know me completely yet, I confess I have difficulty summarizing who I am. But this site is exactly the sticker album of my existence: each page is a little piece of the puzzle. Feel free to put it together.

# Travels

Perhaps my biggest dream in life is to travel to all, or almost all, countries in this world. I say perhaps because each passing month I have a different dream. But traveling is certainly something that brings me great happiness. And even better, besides traveling and getting to know different cultures, I had, and continue to have, the privilege of having lived in some of the countries I visited.

## China

It was the country that welcomed me at 18. I learned to get by on my own, to communicate without mastering the language, and to build a routine in a completely new world. I adapted in six months and understood that the courage to go is the first step for any creation.


## Thailand

Our refuge during the pandemic. In Chiang Mai, silent and almost without tourists, I rediscovered drawing, started studying frontend on my own, and discovered code art tools. Thailand gave me back an artist I thought had been left in childhood. It was the most creative period of my adult life.


## Turkey

It was my home for two and a half years, also during the pandemic. I fell in love with the country's rich history and the friendly, helpful people I met throughout my time there. Bandirma was the city that welcomed me. A small place on the coast of the Marmara sea, with a quiet life and beautiful beaches around. I really enjoyed looking at the olive trees from my home office window and the sunset that flooded the house with vivid orange. I adopted two stray kittens, Piri and Babalu, and ate an uncountable ammount of Ci Kofte.
